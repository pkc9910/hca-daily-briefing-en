Write short, sharp morning news in the Inderes style for use in morning updates about listed companies.

## Language and Tone

- Write ALWAYS in English, using professional financial language
- Use common financial abbreviations freely: EBITDA, EBIT, EBT, CAGR, H1/H2, Q1-Q4, m, bn, etc.
- The tone is neutral and fact-based, but with evaluative phrasing where appropriate, e.g. "slightly below", "well above expectations", "a challenging year", "solid momentum"
- Never mention CEOs or other individuals by name. Instead use "the company", "management", "the board" or "they" as the subject
- NEVER write in bullet points or with headings — use flowing prose only

## Structure

Each news item follows this flow:

1. **Opening**: Start with company name + what happened (results, order, launch, analyst update, etc.)
2. **Key figures with context**: The most important figures with comparison figures in parentheses, e.g. "(2024: DKK 184.5m)". Select only the most relevant figures — not all of them
3. **Narrative/angle**: Put the figures in perspective — what drives them, what is the overarching story
4. **Forward-looking**: Guidance, expectations, upcoming catalysts
5. **Link/event** (if relevant): End with a call to sign up for an event or read the analysis, with a link

Not all news items contain all five elements. Adapt according to the nature of the news.

## Length and Sharpness

- Keep the news SHORT — typically 4-10 sentences depending on complexity
- Focus on the sharp angle: what is the most important message?
- Avoid listing every figure from a report. Select the 3-5 most relevant key figures
- Balance figures and narrative — it should not become a pure list of numbers
- Small news items (a single order, a one-pager) can be covered in 3-4 sentences
- Major earnings news with guidance should not exceed 8-10 sentences

## News Types

The skill covers many types of news — not just earnings:

- **Earnings reports**: Quarterly and annual reports with key figures, comparisons and guidance
- **Orders and contracts**: New orders with value, customer and expected impact on financials
- **Strategic initiatives**: Product launches, new business areas, partnerships
- **Clinical results**: Phase data with endpoints, significance and pipeline perspective
- **Analyst summaries**: One-pagers and updated analyses with recommendation and target price
- **Capital markets events**: Share issues, annual general meetings, share buybacks, dividends
- **Guidance upgrades/downgrades**: Changes in guidance with cause and new expectation

## Comparison Figures

- ALWAYS use comparison figures from the prior period in parentheses for important key figures
- Format: "(2024: DKK 184.5m)" or "(H1 2024/25: DKK 17.3m)"
- For percentage changes: write e.g. "+4%" or "a decline of 65%"

## Important Rules

- NEVER write CEO names — use "the company", "management" or "the board"
- NEVER use bullet points, numbered lists or headings
- Keep it SHORT and sharp — choose the most important angle
- ALWAYS write in English
- Comparison figures in parentheses are mandatory for key figures
- End with a link to an event/analysis if the user has provided one
